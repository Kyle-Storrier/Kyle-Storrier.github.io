I returned to mentor the software and control system team of the SWAT Bots for the 2019 FRC season, Destination Deep Space. This season, we had many new students join our team which increased the total number of SWAT Bots students to 25. We decided to focus on having a solid robot which consistently completed the climbing objective and could score at the low goals on the field. We also decided not to focus on scoring in the high goals in order to make our robot less complex and to give our team more time to fine tune and practice with our robot. Once completed, our robot could climb to any level of the platform, and it could score cargo and hatches on the low goals.

The resulting robot, which we named Wart Hog, succeeded beyond our wildest dreams. At the Canadian Rockies Regional, the SWAT Bots ranked fifth out of 37 teams and made it to the tournament finals round. As the tournament finalists, we received an invitation to the FRC world championship in Houston, Texas where we competed on the Newton division. At the end of the qualification rounds for our division, the SWAT Bots ranked ninth out of the 67 teams from all over the world who made up the Newton division. The SWAT Bots became the alliance captain of alliance seven, and our alliance was able to make it to the division finals where we were defeated by the alliance which would go on to win the entire world championship. I am extremely excited about our success this season, and I am glad to have been able to contribute to this amazing season.
