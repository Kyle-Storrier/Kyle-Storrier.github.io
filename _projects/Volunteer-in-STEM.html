Here are my top reasons to volunteer in Science, Technology, Engineering and Math (STEM) programs. photo: /images/Volunteer-in-STEM/image7.jpg --- Over the last several years, I have had many opportunities to volunteer in various capacities at children's science, technology, engineering, and math (STEM) events, day camps, workshops, and competitions. During this time, I have discovered numerous reasons why everyone should volunteer in children's STEM programs.  I will highlight a few of my favorites in this post.
1. It allows you to share your passion with those following in your footsteps. I have had several opportunities to help out and teach workshops to members of FLL teams, and I mentored FTC team #10015 in the 2015 - 2016 FTC season. During that time, I was able to help several young, talented programmers to continue developing their skills.  In return, they have further ignited my passion for science, technology engineering, and math.
2. It helps develop life skills. For example, my public speaking skills have been greatly enhanced by my experience presenting at the FRC West 2015 Symposium and the 2016 MECHFEST, discussing FIRST with spectators at events, and speaking to potential sponsors as well as judges at competitions. These skills proved extremely valuable for me when I was my high school graduation valedictorian.  I know that my experiences volunteering and teaching at FIRST events will be a huge benefit for my future, and I hope that I have inspired others in the process as well.

3. It gives you an opportunity to help build a community. I have been able to assist in building community by volunteering at a number of FLL events which helped FLL expand in Alberta. Additionally, I helped the SWAT Bots put on a series of Monthly Robotics Challenges which allowed us to spread our love of robotics to our community.
4. It is fun!
