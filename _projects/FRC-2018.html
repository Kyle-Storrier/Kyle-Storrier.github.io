For the 2018 FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) season, FIRST Power Up, I continued to mentor the SWAT Bots. The 2018 challenge required robots to place cubes on balancing goals of varying heights in order to score points. The SWAT Bots’ robot, Shadow, used a wheeled intake to collect the cubes and to launch them into the goals. Shadow’s cube intake mechanism was mounted on a two-stage cascading elevator which could raise and lower the mechanism to score in the vault, switch, and scale.

Unfortunately, the height of the goals made it extremely difficult for the SWAT Bots to test Shadow before the tournament. This forced us to make several changes and corrections at the tournament. We also didn’t have access to a large space to practice our robot driving in. Despite these setbacks, the SWAT Bots made it to the quarterfinals at the Canadian Rockies Regional in Calgary.
